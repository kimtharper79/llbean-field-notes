Strategic Brief

Reposition L.L.Bean for Gen Z urban consumers by transforming outdoor discovery into a collectible, shareable, documentary-driven experience that lives at the intersection of physical and digital space.

The Insight

Gen Z doesn't separate "outdoors" from "urban." They discover nature on rooftops, in parks between classes, on bridges at golden hour. They collect experiences the way previous generations collected gear. A photo print from a vending machine is both a physical artifact and a digital flex — proof they were there.

3-Act Narrative Arc
Act 1 Homesick

Solitary urban moments. The commute, the stoop. Missing something you can't name.

Act 2 Discovery

Finding nature in the city. The park, the rooftop, the market, the bridge. Eyes opening.

Act 3 Community

Individual discovery becomes collective experience. The gather, the promise. We belong here.

How to Navigate

Click any board below to explore the wireframe. Use the top navigation bar or ← → arrow keys to move between views. Each board covers one layer of the system: physical machine, print design, digital activation, gallery, and rare/limited editions.

Field Notes lives at the intersection of documentary photography and collectible culture. The physical print is an artifact of place. The digital collectible is proof of presence.

Eight vending machines placed at urban checkpoint locations dispense limited-edition photo prints. Each print's unique QR code unlocks a digital collectible card with documentary story content. Collect all eight to complete the narrative arc — from solitary commute to community gathering.
