Part 1 — Concept Overview

“Field Notes”

A dual collectible campaign repositioning L.L.Bean for Gen Z urban consumers

SCAD — Advertising & Branding Kimberly Harper-Colucci ADBR Campaign Concept · Winter 2026
The Problem

Gen Z sees L.L.Bean as their parents' brand — built for backcountry weekends they don't take. But this generation doesn't separate “outdoors” from “urban.” Their nature is the park between classes, the rooftop at golden hour, the bridge walk home. L.L.Bean needs a way into these moments — one that feels collectible, shareable, and native to how Gen Z already moves through their city.

The Insight

Gen Z collects experiences, not gear. A photo print from a vending machine is both a physical artifact and a digital flex — proof they were there. The collectible format turns a brand touchpoint into a personal story worth sharing.

Campaign Pillars — Brand Alignment

Storytelling

Rooted in L.L.Bean's tradition of documentary storytelling. Each print captures a real urban moment — no stock photography, no studio shoots. The collection tells a complete narrative arc from solitude to community.

Innovative Technology

QR-activated digital collectibles bridge physical and digital. NFC-enabled contactless payment. Thermal sublimation printing for archival-quality output. Web-based AR layer for enhanced story content.

Brand Content Integration

L.L.Bean's outdoor heritage meets urban Gen Z culture. The vending machine becomes a brand artifact. The collection mechanic drives repeat engagement. Every interaction is shareable content.

Edition System

Each location produces 50 standard prints + 10 rare prints (gold border, alternate angle). 400 standard + 80 rare = 480 total prints across the campaign. When the community collectively reaches 500 activations, a collaborative community card unlocks for everyone.
